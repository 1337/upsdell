A word from the former Upsdell house captain
Upsdell came into the 2006-2007 school year, as they say, ready to rock: having given the other houses their time to shine after winning a tremendous streak of three years in a row, Upsdell looked enthused and back in fighting form for the ultimate House Cup. Though the beginning of the year saw some lack of success, it is perhaps Upsdell’s perseverance that has pulled us back in to the thick of the House competition!

Upsdell brought their spirit and enthusiasm to the first big event of the year: the Swimming Gala. With face paint, hair dye, ribbons and banners, the House of Red brimmed with enthusiasm, cheering as loud as possible for our rouge-capped swimmers as they sped across the pool in Kowloon Tsai. Unfortunately, even the cheery cheerleaders’ chants and cool dance moves couldn’t change the results, as Upsdell finished in fourth place.

Despite the outstanding participation that Upsdellians showed (even if it did involve the house captains to drag unwilling victims onto the field to run), our House did not place any better in the Cross Country, coming fourth again.

Upsdell’s fortunes were to take a pivotal turn in November, when we sang, danced, and played our instruments during the Inter-house Music competition. With our delightfully cute Juniors singing All Star, our Seniors doing That Thing You Do, as well as our individually talented musicians strutting their stuff, we were confident that we’d done considerably well. It was then when Upsdell managed to move up to third place. We managed to achieve even better for Inter-house Chess, coming a very respectable second.

Following the Christmas and New Year Break, Upsdell came out swinging; or shall we say, running, throwing, and jumping! Sports Day being one of our stronger events in the past 5 years, Upsdell was ready to redeem ourselves for past misfortunes. The outstanding participation in this event, as well as our naturally talented runners, brought us all the way up to come in second place! A great effort by all Upsdellians!

We weren’t done yet. In March, Upsdellians danced their way into their first House victory in the Evening of Music and Dance. After countless hours of practices, rehearsals and a lot of hard work, Upsdell boys and girls managed to create the best dances of the competition. Whether it was the cute and delightful ‘date at the movies’ dance by the Juniors, or the Seniors’ eerily synchronized fast-paced moves, Upsdell stunned the crowd with their originality, skills and energy! Well done to all those who danced, as well as a special mention to the dance captains, who put in so much time and effort to coordinate one of the biggest competitions of the year.

Though the year started off sluggish, a strong house spirit and perseverance saw Upsdell pull together before the end of the year and show some of our true colours (or shall we say, colour, i.e. red!) We wish Upsdell the best of luck in next year’s competition! GO UPSDELL GO!

Jerry Choi

From the Lion yearbook 2007, Page 41
