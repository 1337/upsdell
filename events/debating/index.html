Debating
Rules

Format

  • Debates will be ‘points of information debates’
  • There will be three speakers in each team
  • Each speaker has up to three minutes
  • There will be a warning bell 15 seconds before 3 minutes
  • More than 30 seconds over time will be penalized
  • Each speaker will have one minute before p.o.i. can be offered
  • At least one p.o.i. should be accepted (3 maximum)
  • No speaker in the opposing team should offer more than one p.o.i.

Rules

  • The motion will be emailed the morning before the debate is scheduled.
  • Teams should be at the venue, ready to begin at 1.40pm.
  • Teams must include three students from the team list.
  • If one team is not there at 1.40pm, the other team will receive a walkover.

Scoring

  • Each speaker will be awarded a mark out of 100.
  • This will be based on: Matter 40, Manner 40, Method 20.
  • Matter is the effectiveness of the case line and the supporting argument
  • Speaker one should establish the overall case line and outline the general areas to be discussed by the following two speakers. (there should not be repetition of the same points – each speaker should have a distinct contribution to make to the argument of his team)
  • Speaker two should include some rebuttal of the argument made by the opposing side.
  • Speaker three should further refute the opposition’s case line in a summing up.
  • Manner is the way in which speakers deliver their speeches. This includes the way you look, the fluency of your speech and the effectiveness of the language you use.
  • Method is the organization of your speech.  This includes the organization of a speech within itself and as part of the overall team case.  It concerns not only what was planned beforehand, but also what has to be dealt with during the debate.
  • A losing team will score one point.
  • A winning team will score two or three points depending on the margin of victory.
  • (If a team scores 10% more than its opponents, it will score three points.)
  • The top teams in each ‘league’ will compete in the final.  The points will be doubled in the final.  There will be a best speaker in the competition (with the highest points average).
  • A walkover will score three points.
